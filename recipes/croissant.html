48-layers Croissant

  • Back

      • Ingredients

      Dough

    • flour 250g
    • water 150mL
    • yeast 3g
    • salt 1 tsp
    • sugar 1tbsp
    • softened butter 10g

      • Butter inside

    • butter 100g

      Process

    1. Mix up all ingredients except for butter and knead until smooth.
    2. Add in butter and knead. The dough should preferably pass the windowpane test.
    3. Once the dough is nice and smooth, cover and rest in the fridge for 30 minutes.
    4. Put about 100g of cold butter in a plastic bag or a piece of parchment paper.
    5. Roll out the butter into a rectangle and freeze for 20 minutes.
    6. Once the butter is cold and hard again, take the dough out and roll it out into a rectangle 1.5 times the size of the butter.
    7. Place the butter in the center of the dough and close the dough up.
    8. Put the closed dough in the freezer for another 20 minutes.
    9. Repeat these two steps for another three times.
    10. Take out the dough and roll it out to a bigger, thinner sheet.
    11. Divide the dough into stripes
    12. Begin rolling.
    13. Once the individual little croissants are rolled up, cover loosely with plastic wrap and let rise for 1 hour.
    14. Brush on some egg wash.
    15. Bake in preheated oven at 200℃ for 15 to 20 minutes.

      Things to read before you start making this recipe

      Remember to rest the croissants at a cool temperature before baking since butter WILL melt at about 30℃.

      The folding technique is the same as the dirty buns, see my previous note.
