Cinnamon Rolls

  • Back

      • Ingredients

    • 450g all purpose flour
    • 7g yeast
    • Some sugar and salt
    • 260g milk
    • A splash of olive oil
    • Some softened butter, enough to form a paste with the brown sugar and cinnamon
    • 5 tbsp dark brown sugar
    • 2 tbsp cinnamon powder

      Process

    1. Form the first five ingredients to a dough. Knead for 10 minutes, then cover and let rise until doubled in size.
    2. When the dough is rising, combine butter, brown sugar, and cinnamon to form a spreadable paste.
    3. Roll the dough out into a perfect rectangle that is about 7mm thick. This is probebly the most challenging part of this recipe.
    4. Spread the paste on the rectangle dough, make sure to cover every corner.
    5. Roll the rectangle up to a log.
    6. Cut up the log evenly and place them in an oiled baking pan. Don't let the doughs touch each other.
    7. Cover and let rise for another 40 minutes.
    8. Bake in a preheated oven at 180℃ for 25 to 30 minutes.
    9. This step is optional but you can drizzle on some icing (powdered sugar and water) when the rolls are cooled down. But I highly recommend not to because the rolls themselves are sweet enough.

      Things to read before you start making this recipe

      You can roll the rectangle up either from left to right or from top to bottom, it really doesn't make a big difference.

      Don't tightly pack your rolls in a baking pan since they will rise in the oven and might not cook evenly.
