Steamed Twisted Rolls

  • Back

      • Ingredients

    • Flour 500g
    • Yeast 4g
    • Water 290g
    • Scallions, chopped
    • Oil 20g
    • Some salt

      Process

    1. Mix the first three ingredients to a dough. Rest the dough for 10 minutes and knead until smooth.
    2. Oil the bowl. Cover and proof for 1 hour.
    3. Take out the dough and deflate it. Roll the dough out to a rectangle that is not too thin.
    4. Spread the scallion-oil-salt mixture evenly on top of the rectangle.
    5. Fold the rectangle in thirds lengthwise, press the dough gently between folds to deflate the air. You should get a thin rectangle with the same length, but 1/3 of the width.
    6. Cut strips that are about 2cm wide along the length of the thin rectangle.
    7. Take two strips, lay one on top of the other and press in the middle with a chopstick.
    8. Gently stretch the strip longer and flip it up-side-down (so the side that's pressed by the chopstick is on the table).
    9. Place a chopstick in the middle of the long strip and fold the strip in half.
    10. Tightly pinch the endpoint and twist the chopstick twice.
    11. Press the chopstick down and take it out. You will get a twisted roll.
    12. Put it on a steam cooker/bamboo steamer and place it on top of a pot of COLD water. Don't forget to oil the surface of the cooker so the rolls won't stick
    13. Steam for 15 minutes when the water starts to boil and you can see steam coming out.
    14. After 15 minutes, turn off the heat, but DO NOT OPEN THE LID. The rolls will deflate if you open it immediately.

      Things to read before you start making this recipe

      Leave room for the rolls to grow when you put them in the cooker.

      The scallion is optional. If you want to make the rolls in its original flavor, spread some oil instead of the scallion mixture in step 4
